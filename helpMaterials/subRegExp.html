How to use negative and positive Lookahead and Lookbehind?

Lookahead and lookbehind are very usefull if we want check for something to be exisitng or not and at the same time we don't need that thing

Negative Lookahead it will check for what to be excluded. In the example we want the word "Cat" not to be followed by number 2 and Cat2 is not matched by the regular expression.

/Cat(?!2)/g

Cat1 Cat2 CatZ

Positive Lookahead it will check for what to be existing after the thing we search. In the example we want the word "Cat" to be followed by number 2 and only Cat2 is matched by the regular expression.

/Cat(?=2)/g

Cat1 Cat2 CatZ

Negative Lookbehind it will check for what not to be before our thing. In the example we want the word "Cat" not to have by number 2 before and Cat2 is not matched by the regular expression.

/(?<!2)Cat/g

1Cat 2Cat ZCat

Positive Lookbehind it will check for what to be before our thing. In the example we want the word "Cat" to have number 2 before and only Cat2 is matched by the regular expression.

/(?<=2)Cat/g

1Cat 2Cat ZCat
